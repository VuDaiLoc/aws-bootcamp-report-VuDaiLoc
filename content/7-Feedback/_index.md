> Here, you can freely share your personal opinions about your experience participating in the First Cloud AI Journey program. This will help the FCAJ team improve any shortcomings based on the following aspects:

### Overall Evaluation

**1. Working Environment**  
The environment is pretty friendly and open, people are willing to help when I get stuck, even outside working hours. The workspace itself is comfortable and easy to focus in. If I could suggest one thing, it'd be having a few more social/team-bonding sessions, since right now most of the interaction is purely work-related.

**2. Support from Mentor / Team Admin**  
The mentor explains things clearly when I don't get something, but also lets me try to figure it out myself first — which I actually found useful, since it forced me to get better at debugging instead of just relying on being handed the answer. The admin team handled paperwork and logistics smoothly, no real complaints there.

**3. Relevance of Work to Academic Major**  
What I was assigned lines up reasonably well with what I studied, but there was also a lot that school never really covers in depth (cloud/AWS especially), so it was a mix of reinforcing old knowledge and learning a bunch of new stuff.

**4. Learning & Skill Development Opportunities**  
This is probably where I gained the most — actually using AWS services in a real setup, debugging deployment issues on my own, and getting better at explaining my work clearly. The mentor also shared a lot of real-world experience that gave me a better sense of direction while I'm still figuring out my next steps.

**5. Company Culture & Team Spirit**  
People take work seriously but it doesn't feel overly tense — when something's urgent, everyone pitches in instead of pushing tasks onto each other. Even as an intern, I felt treated like an actual part of the team.

**6. Internship Policies / Benefits**  
There's an internship allowance and reasonable flexibility with hours when needed. Getting to join internal training sessions was a nice bonus too, added some knowledge outside of just my assigned work.

### Additional Questions
- What did you find **most satisfying** during your internship?  
  Getting the whole pipeline (OCR → AI summarization → DynamoDB → real-time UI updates) finally working end-to-end, after being stuck on deployment errors for a good while — that moment stuck with me.

- What do you think the company **should improve** for future interns?  
  Clearer documentation upfront about service quotas and access limits (like for Bedrock), so interns spend less time blindly guessing while still getting to figure most things out themselves.

- If recommending to a friend, would you **suggest they intern here**? Why or why not?  
  Yes — you actually get to work with real AWS infrastructure instead of just theory, and the mentor support is solid when you need it.

---

### Suggestions & Expectations
- Do you have any suggestions to improve the internship experience?  
  More interaction between interns, and maybe a quick-reference doc for common infrastructure errors.

- Would you like to continue this program in the future?  
  Still deciding — I might lean toward cybersecurity after FCAJ wraps up, but this experience is a solid foundation either way.

- Any other comments (free sharing):  
  Thanks to the team for sticking with me through the 12 weeks, especially the stretches where I got stuck on deployment issues for a while.
