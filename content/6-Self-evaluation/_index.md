During my internship at **Amazon Web Services Vietnam**, under the **First Cloud AI Journey (FCAJ) Program**, from **[20/4/2026]** to **[31/7/2026]**, I got to put what I learned in school into an actual project, and honestly picked up a lot of things classes never really cover.

I was assigned to build the **"AI-Powered Smart Document Assistant"**, using **Angular 18+** for the front-end and **AWS Amplify Gen 2** on the backend, together with services like **Cognito, S3, DynamoDB, AppSync/GraphQL, Lambda, Textract, SNS, and Amazon Bedrock** to handle document OCR, AI summarization, and real-time UI updates. Along the way I ran into plenty of deployment issues (CloudFormation errors, IAM permission gaps, Bedrock quota limits...) and had to dig through them mostly on my own, so my debugging skills improved a lot compared to before the internship. I also got better at writing technical documentation, since I had to summarize my work every week.

As for work ethic, I tried to get my tasks done properly, followed the company's rules, and reached out to others when I got stuck instead of struggling alone for too long.

### Needs Improvement

* Need to be more disciplined about schedules and processes — I still work in bursts based on mood sometimes
* When I hit an unfamiliar error (especially AWS infrastructure ones), I tend to struggle with it alone for too long before asking for help — need to reach out sooner
* My communication can be a bit roundabout at times; need to work on getting to the point faster, both day-to-day and professionally
